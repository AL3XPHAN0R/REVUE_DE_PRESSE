# Persona & Target Audience (Layer 3 — Reference)

## Author
Alexandre Phanor — future licensed personal insurance broker (life, disability, critical illness — LLQP/PQAP) in Quebec, Canada. Bilingual, French-first. Creating Instagram and podcast content that demystifies personal insurance for entrepreneurs.

**Content mission:** own the *problems* of income protection and business continuity before the solution is needed — not product-selling content, but problem-owning content. The audience should think of Alexandre when the problem surfaces, not when they shop for insurance.

## Product scope (the 4 problems the content owns)
 
1. **Personal disability insurance** — the owner's income stops, nothing replaces it
2. **Business overhead expense insurance** — fixed costs keep burning during the owner's disability
3. **Key-person insurance** — loss of value when a key employee or partner disappears
4. **Funded buy-sell agreement** — ownership transfer at death/disability of a partner

## Target audience

**Primary:**
- Canadian SME owners (2–50 employees)
- Self-employed professionals and freelancers
- Entrepreneurs without group benefits
- Business partners operating without (or with unfunded) shareholder agreements
- Business owners exposed to key-person risk
- Founders in early-stage companies (no safety net)

**Secondary (referral network — content to share WITH them):**
- CPAs, corporate/estate lawyers, wealth managers, business brokers

### Direct themes (the obvious problem each product solves)
- Disability insurance / income protection for the self-employed (incl. RRQ/EI gaps for entrepreneurs)
- Business overhead expense coverage; fixed costs during owner illness
- Key-person coverage; cost of losing/replacing a key employee
- Buy-sell agreements and their funding; death/disability of a business partner
- Critical illness and life insurance for business owners
- Group benefits for small businesses (as a gateway topic to personal coverage gaps)
- Disability incidence statistics; mental health and chronic illness as leading claim causes
- Coverage-gap statistics among Canadian entrepreneurs and SMEs
### Indirect themes (adjacent angles — same problem, no product mention)
- **Succession / relève:** SME succession deficit in Quebec/Canada (FCEI/CFIB, CTEQ data), intergenerational transfer, estate freeze, family trusts, family business transitions (success and failure stories)
- **Founder dependence:** bus factor, "would your business survive your 6-month absence," delegation, process documentation, business value discounted by owner-dependence
- **Owner health:** entrepreneur burnout, mental load, health and longevity of business leaders
- **Cash flow & resilience:** SME cash reserves/runway, financial fragility, fixed-cost pressure, bank covenants and personal guarantees on business loans
- **Partner relationships & governance:** shareholder agreements (shotgun clauses, right of first refusal), partner disputes, divorce/retirement of a partner, business valuation methods and disagreements
- **Tax & estate (referral-network gold):** tax at death of a business owner (deemed disposition, capital dividend account, lifetime capital gains exemption), estate liquidity, salary vs dividends and its impact on insurability
- **Talent:** retention of key employees, deferred compensation, cost of turnover for critical roles
- **Psychology:** entrepreneur optimism bias, underinvestment in resilience vs growth, low-probability/high-impact risk
### Regulatory & industry
- AMF / CSF / ChAD / CIRO regulation affecting personal insurance distribution (Quebec and Canada)
- Insurtech/fintech trends affecting SME and individual coverage

## Selection priority
- Direct themes and regulatory/industry items come first — they are the daily backbone of the digest.
- Indirect themes add variety and podcast-worthy story angles but should stay a minority of the 5–8 articles selected (roughly no more than half), not overtake the direct/product-adjacent content.
- Tie-breaker when relevance is otherwise similar: prefer podcast-worthy angles — concrete stories, case studies, or surprising statistics — over generic or purely descriptive coverage.

## Exclusion criteria — EXCLUDE articles about
- General politics with no insurance or SME-risk angle
- B2C mass-market insurance (auto, home, travel)
- P&C commercial insurance (property, liability, cyber) unless directly tied to business continuity or owner risk
- Generic macroeconomic news (inflation, interest rates) unless tied to SME financial fragility or protection gaps
- Corporate/enterprise insurance (>500 employees)
- Pure investment/portfolio/markets content with no protection or continuity angle
- Product marketing or carrier press releases with no editorial substance
- Topics with no clear relevance to personal insurance, SME protection, or business continuity
